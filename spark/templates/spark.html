WHAT IS THE SPARK CHALLENGE?

The Spark Challenge is a campus-wide, corporate-sponsored design competition, hosted by the School of Electrical and Computer Engineering in partnership with the ECE Student Society and General Motors.

The Spark Challenge is open to all undergraduate students of Purdue University. It allows students of any discipline to present their projects to the rest of campus and many corporate sponsors. This results in networking with representatives from some of the top leading industries in the nation, acting as a gateway to prospective employers.

This event is the chance for students from any and all majors to collaborate in developing various projects and show off their technical skills.

REGISTRATION

THE PROJECT MUST MEET THE FOLLOWING REQUIREMENTS!
• The project must utilize at least one microcontroller, CPLD, or FPGA.
• Final product must be fully functional and packaged to prototyped stage.
• Each team must have a team captain and a maximum of four members.
• At least one team member must be present during the event.
• A project poster of size 36"x48" is required
